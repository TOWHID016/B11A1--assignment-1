**Bangladesh 2.0: Yunus Leads Reforms** Following the ouster of Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina in a student-led revolution, Nobel laureate Muhammad Yunus has assumed leadership to rebuild Bangladesh. Protests against inequality and political repression left over 1,500 dead, with Hasina fleeing to India. Yunus’s interim government faces challenges, including institutional paralysis, slow reforms, and accusations of Islamist influence. Efforts focus on drafting a new constitution, tackling corruption, and restoring economic stability amid political uncertainty. Concerns over minority rights, women’s representation, and press freedom persist. Yunus aims to enact lasting reforms, ensuring democracy and prosperity for a “Bangladesh 2.0” that thrives beyond this tumultuous transition.
